FirstPass: AI Contract Triage for In-House Legal Teams

Every contract gets an AI first pass. A human confirms. The queue gets shorter every month.

THE PROBLEM: Most contracts are 80% boilerplate, yet every single one gets a full cold read by a scarce, expensive human. The queue backs up at end of quarter. There is no risk-based triage.

THE SOLUTION: FirstPass is an agentic triage layer that reads NDAs and order forms, checks them against the legal team's playbook of standard positions, and routes each one as GREEN (auto-clear), AMBER (confirm pre-drafted redlines), or RED (escalate to a human). Every human decision feeds back into the playbook, so the queue gets shorter every month.

KEY METRICS:
- 0 unsafe misses: nothing risky was auto-cleared
- 100% triage verdict accuracy
- 93% material-issue recall (no LLM)
- 58% of human review time removed
- ~73 legal hours/month saved at 200 contracts/month

HOW IT WORKS:
1. Ingest the contract file
2. Classify the type and extract key terms
3. Retrieve the team's standard positions from the playbook
4. Detect red flags, deviations, missing clauses, and novel terms
5. Score findings into GREEN / AMBER / RED
6. Draft the exact redline the team has used before
7. Route the decision
8. LEARN: the lawyer's resolution becomes a playbook entry

PRIVATE BY DESIGN: The deterministic engine needs no model at all. The optional reasoning layer runs on a local open-weight model (Hermes 3) on the institution's own server. No contract text ever leaves the building.

THE FLYWHEEL: When a lawyer resolves a novel RED case, it becomes a playbook entry. Next time it is AMBER with the redline pre-drafted. Every human decision permanently shrinks the future queue.
